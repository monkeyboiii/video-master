## Title variants (ranked; label each with its opening formula A/B/C/D)

1. "You don't stay in the same gear." — formula: **B identity contrast** — why: it is the line the
   whole video turns on, it is rider language before it is product language, and it makes the
   viewer finish the sentence themselves.
2. "The part of DirtBikeX you can't see yet." — formula: D counterintuitive — why: names the
   members-only post whose text is literally blurred on screen; curiosity, not a discount.
3. "Four things you get for being early." — formula: C benefit promise — why: concrete and
   countable, and the video delivers exactly four.
4. "I built a paid tier and I'll tell you not to buy it." — formula: D counterintuitive — why: the
   closing line is "you don't need this to enjoy the community", and that is the most honest thing
   in the video. Riskiest, and the most on-brand.
