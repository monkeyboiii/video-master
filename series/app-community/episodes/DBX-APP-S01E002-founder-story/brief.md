## Viewer (one concrete person)

A dirt bike rider whose passion is invisible to the people around them — a partner,
coworkers, old friends who don't get it. They ride (or want to) but have no one to share
it with and no real home for it online. Same viewer as E001; here we reach them through
**story**, not a punchy teaser.

## Pain point, in the viewer's words

- en-US: "I love this and nobody around me gets it — there's no real place for riders like me."
- zh-CN: deferred (en-US first).

## One-sentence viewer benefit (the core conclusion this video proves)

A rider built the home he wished existed — so you finally have people who get it, and a
place that's actually made for you.

## Topic formula (one line)

Pain (no one around me gets my passion / no home for it) + solution (a rider built a
global community for exactly this) + benefit (you belong, and it's made for you).

## Designed reactions (the judgment test — all four must have real answers)

- Like because: the story names a feeling they've never heard said out loud.
- Save because: weak for a story — not a primary goal (don't force a fake checklist).
- Comment because: invite them to share their own "nobody gets it" moment — or reuse
  E001's comment-for-invite-code mechanic.
- Follow / download because: they want in on what the founder is building; the ending
  designs that expectation.

## Proposed structure — FOR DISCUSSION (not locked)

Longer arc than E001's teaser; every beat still passes the golden-rules retention bar
(≤2s hook, no self-intro, positioning embedded, framed as viewer benefit, designed
ending). The user's shape: **hook → my why-story → light features → CTA.**

1. **Hook** (≤2s) — a scroll-stopper that opens the story (TBD: a line, a confession, a strong visual).
2. **The problem, from the viewer's side** — the isolation/dismissal, told so the viewer feels "that's me."
3. **My why (founder story)** — why I built it: my own version of that problem, embedded as positioning ("I felt this too, so…"), not a bio.
4. **How it solves it** — the community as the answer to *their* problem (belonging), framed as viewer benefit, not "an app that does X."
5. **Light features** — 2–3 features touched briefly as proof the solution is real (NOT the E001 phone-montage centerpiece).
6. **Ending** — designed follow expectation + one action (download / comment).
