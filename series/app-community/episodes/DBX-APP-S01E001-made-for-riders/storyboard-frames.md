# Storyboard Frame Prompts — DBX-APP-S01E001 (made-for-riders)

Hand these to an image-generation agent to produce **9:16 previz boards — one static
frame per shot** — so you can block "where to stand" and drop placeholder stills onto a
timeline to test the edit (pacing, transitions, music, scratch VO) **before filming
anything**. Seven frames, SH010–SH070, ~17s total. This is a previz aid, not a
pipeline-gated artifact — the real overlays (`subtitle-track`, the `feature-callout`
component, `cta-card`) replace the baked-in text in the actual edit.

## Global rules — apply to EVERY frame (prepend to each prompt, or paste the whole sheet)

- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical, **1080×1920**.
- **Style:** cinematic photoreal previz still — looks like a real phone-shot frame grab,
  natural light, believable. (Swap to "rough black-and-white marker storyboard sketch"
  if you'd rather have classic boards.)
- **Safe zone:** keep the subject and ALL on-screen text inside the central safe area —
  leave the top ~8%, bottom ~22%, and right ~13% clear (that's where the platform UI
  sits). On-screen text goes in the lower third, above the bottom margin.
- **Brand (app/graphic frames):** warm near-black background (#16140F), dirt-orange
  accent (#ED6B00), clean modern sans-serif, bold "X"-mark logo. Modern, minimal,
  high-contrast — no clutter, no stock-photo gloss.
- **Consistency:** lock ONE reference for the founder (a photo or a fixed seed) and
  reuse it for every founder frame; lock ONE app-UI style and reuse it for all
  app-screen frames, so the mockups read as one product.
- **On-frame text:** each board includes its on-screen line as a simple placeholder so
  the still reads on its own in the animatic (the animated overlay replaces it later).
- **Naming:** save as `DBX-APP-S01E001_SH###_board_v001.png` (e.g.
  `..._SH010_board_v001.png`) so each frame maps to its shot on the timeline.

Only **two live-action setups to block**: the doubter (SH010) and the founder (SH020 —
same position also delivers the SH030 pivot line). SH030–SH060 are app-UI mockups;
SH070 is a graphic.

## SH010 — hook · the doubter  (hold ~2.0s)

> 9:16 vertical photoreal previz still, 1080×1920. A frustrated woman in her 30s standing
> in an ordinary home kitchen or hallway, caught mid-yell with both hands thrown up in
> exasperation, looking off toward the camera side. Handheld, slightly tilted, raw
> phone-video energy, plain available indoor light, mundane domestic background. Framed
> waist-up, just right of center. Overlay text, lower third: "Nobody cares about your
> dirt bike!". Mood: real, unpolished, comedic frustration.

## SH020 — "I do" · founder  (hold ~1.5s)

> 9:16 vertical photoreal previz still, 1080×1920. The DirtBikeX founder (man, late
> 20s–30s) in a medium close-up, centered, dead-calm neutral expression, looking
> straight into the lens. Simple modern interior or clean dark backdrop, soft directional
> key light. Head-and-shoulders inside the central safe area, eyes on the upper-third
> line. Overlay text, lower third: "I do.". Understated, confident, deadpan. Use the
> locked founder reference.

## Once you have the seven boards

Drop them on V1 at the hold times above (≈17s total), lay the scratch VO on A1 and a
music bed on A2, and you have a full animatic to test transitions, the whip into the
app, the montage pace, and the music/SFX sync — no footage required. What survives that
test becomes the shot list you actually film.
