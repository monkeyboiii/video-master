## Title variants (ranked; each leads with pain / contrast / benefit / counterintuitive)

1. "Don't buy the 450. Your first dirt bike is smaller than you think" — why: leads with
   the exact purchase the viewer is eyeing tonight (pain + counterintuitive), and names
   the video's on-record claim, so the promise is paid off in full.
2. "The first-bike mistake that makes new riders quit" — why: pain/fear framing with a
   curiosity gap; strong CTR but weaker than #1 because it hides the concrete answer, and
   the golden rules favor promises we cash immediately.
3. "Fit beats power: how to pick a first dirt bike you'll actually ride" — why: pure
   benefit statement, honest and searchable, but no tension — best as YouTube Shorts
   title where search intent matters more than scroll-stopping.

## Cover concept

- Background image intent: beginner-height rider sitting on a mid-size trail bike, both
  feet flat on the ground, garage/trailhead setting — next to (or ghosted behind) a
  too-tall 450 MX bike on tiptoes. The visual is the argument: flat feet vs tiptoes.
- On-cover text (≤2 lines, legible at thumbnail size): "FIRST BIKE?" / "DON'T BUY THE 450"
- Aspect(s) needed per platform: tiktok 9x16 (1080x1920); reels 9x16 with text inside the
  central 3x4 grid crop; ytshorts — none (no cover upload; see first frame below).
- Emotional read at a glance: "I was about to make an expensive mistake" — relief that
  someone is finally telling a beginner the unglamorous truth.

## First frame (0.0s)

Rider on tiptoes wobbling on a tall 450 at a stop, shot from behind at ankle height —
feet straining to reach the ground. Overlay word "THE 450 TRAP" punches in with the first
spoken line. This frame doubles as the YouTube Shorts thumbnail (frame-select at upload —
Shorts has no cover upload, so this composed frame IS the cover there).
