# Golden Rules — the DirtBikeX video quality bar

Every video is judged against this document. It applies to both language variants and
every platform. When a rule here conflicts with convenience, the rule wins; when it
genuinely conflicts with a platform norm, record the exception in the episode's
`edit-notes.md`.

## The core principle

A DirtBikeX video does not express what we want to say. It solves a concrete problem for
a rider, track owner, brand, or community member — and it is engineered to produce viewer
reactions, in this order:

```text
留下来 retention → 看得懂 understanding → 收藏 saving → 评论 commenting → 关注 following
```

If you cannot name which reaction a segment is designed to trigger, cut the segment.

## The five design dimensions

Score every topic, script, and cut against all five:

| Dimension | 中文 | The question it answers |
|-----------|------|------------------------|
| User relevance | 用户相关 | Whose concrete problem does this solve? Name the person. |
| Emotional trigger | 情绪触发 | What does the viewer feel in the first 5 seconds — pain, surprise, desire, indignation? |
| Information density | 信息密度 | Does every sentence move the viewer forward? Zero filler. |
| Personal positioning | 人设定位 | Why us — what riding/track/community experience backs this up? |
| Interaction conversion | 互动转化 | What is the viewer explicitly given to save, comment on, or follow for? |

A topic that scores weak on two or more dimensions is not shot. Fix the angle or drop it.

## Stage rules

### Topic (选题)

- Start from a user pain point, never from "what we want to talk about".
- One episode = one problem = one core conclusion. Split anything bigger.
- The viewer benefit must be stateable in one sentence before scripting starts.

### Packaging (封面/标题)

- Title, cover, and first frame are one promise, stated three ways. They must agree.
- The cover is a packaging **hypothesis** written before the script, but locked only
  after the edit — the best first frame often comes from real footage.
- No clickbait the video doesn't cash: the hook promise must be paid off inside the video.

### Hook (开头 2 秒)

- The first 2 seconds must land one of: **pain point, contrast, benefit, or
  counterintuitive claim.**
- Never open with self-introduction, logos, channel branding, or "today we're going to…".
- The first subtitle line and the first spoken line are written as one unit.

### Body (正文)

- Every sentence moves information forward. If a sentence can be deleted with no loss,
  delete it.
- Personal experience appears only in service of the viewer's benefit — proof, not
  autobiography.
- Structure beats improvisation: hook → problem → core conclusion → key points →
  experience proof → checklist → future expectation. (See `skills/03-script-writing.md`.)

### Subtitles (字幕)

- Subtitles **highlight and reinforce** — they do not merely transcribe the voiceover.
  Key numbers, verbs, and conclusions get emphasis; connective tissue can be dropped.
- Keep density readable: zh-CN ≤ ~15 characters per line, en-US ≤ ~38 characters per
  line, one to two lines max on 9:16.
- Subtitles must survive platform UI: keep them inside the safe zone
  (see [platforms.md](platforms.md)).

### Ending (结尾)

- End by creating a future expectation — the concrete reason to follow ("next episode:
  the 3 clutch drills that fix this in one weekend").
- The CTA asks for **one** action, matched to the content: save for checklists, comment
  for opinions/questions, follow for series continuity.

## Localization bar

- zh-CN and en-US are sibling variants: same core message, beats, and visual system;
  independently written hooks, examples, idioms, pacing, and captions.
- A variant that reads like a translation fails review. See [localization.md](localization.md).

## Hard rules (non-negotiable)

1. No video ships without a hook that lands in ≤ 2 seconds.
2. No self-introduction openings, ever.
3. No filler sentences — information density is the house style.
4. Subtitles emphasize; they never just repeat.
5. Every video names its viewer and pays off its promise.
6. Every video ends with a designed follow expectation.
7. Every published video gets a `review.md` with real metrics before the next episode of
   that series is scripted.
